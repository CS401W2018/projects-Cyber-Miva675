QUESTIONS

  • What is the difference between block tags and inline tags?

    • Block tags create elements that are responsible for the structure of texts such as starting on a new line and taking up the full width, while incline tags create elements that stay on the same line and takes up only neessary space often used for small text styling and linking.

  • What is the margin, border, and padding?

    • Margin is the space outside an element, separating it from other texts and elements, a border is the line around an element, text, or image, defining its edge, and a Padding is the space inside an element, between the content and the border.

  • What are units of measurement you can use in CSS?

    • CSS uses absolute and relative units for measurements. Units like 'px' (pixels) and 'cm'(centimeters) are absolute units which have fixed sizes, while units like '%' (percentage) and 'em' (relative to font size) are relative units which are adjusted based on elements.

  • When would you use a figure tag?

    • Figure tags are used to group images, diagrams, or illustrations with their captions, making them easier to style and work with contents. It often allow paddings, borders and margins for the image or diagram inside the box elemnet.
